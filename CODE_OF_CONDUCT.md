# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

Lovable Cookbook is built around critical review, evidence, and practical usefulness. Disagreement is expected. Personal hostility is not.

Participants are expected to:

- discuss ideas, contributions, and risks directly and constructively;
- distinguish facts, assumptions, preferences, and speculation;
- explain criticism clearly instead of dismissing people or their work;
- respect different levels of experience and different technical backgrounds;
- accept correction and revise claims when evidence contradicts them;
- protect private information, credentials, unpublished work, and personal data;
- keep discussions relevant to the project and its goals.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following behavior is not tolerated:

- harassment, threats, intimidation, stalking, or sustained personal attacks;
- discriminatory or demeaning language based on identity or personal characteristics;
- sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted attention;
- doxxing or publishing private information without explicit permission;
- deliberately exposing credentials, secrets, customer data, or private project material;
- spam, impersonation, manipulation, or coordinated disruption;
- knowingly false claims presented as verified facts;
- repeated bad-faith arguments after maintainers have set a clear boundary;
- retaliation against someone who reports a concern in good faith.

## Constructive criticism

Strong criticism is allowed when it is directed at the work.

Good criticism should identify:

1. the concrete problem;
2. the evidence or reasoning behind the concern;
3. the likely consequence;
4. a better alternative, when practical.

“Bad idea” is not useful by itself. “This workflow assumes every project uses Supabase, which makes it unsafe as a general recipe; move the provider-specific steps into an optional section” is useful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, and comments;
- project-related communication in external channels;
- public representation of the project;
- private communication when it directly affects participation in the project.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository owner through GitHub rather than escalating them in a public thread.

Include:

- where the behavior occurred;
- what happened;
- relevant links, screenshots, or timestamps;
- whether there is an immediate safety or privacy risk.

Do not publish sensitive personal information as part of a report.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove content, close or lock discussions, reject contributions, issue warnings, or temporarily or permanently restrict participation.

Enforcement decisions will consider severity, context, prior behavior, impact, and whether the person accepts responsibility and changes their conduct.

Maintainers are expected to handle reports as privately and consistently as reasonably possible.

## Attribution

This policy is inspired by established open-source community standards but is written specifically for the review-oriented nature of Lovable Cookbook.
